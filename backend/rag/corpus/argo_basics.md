# ARGO Float Basics

Argo floats are autonomous instruments that drift with ocean currents and repeatedly profile the water column.
Most core floats cycle every ~10 days, descending to parking depth, drifting, then ascending while recording pressure,
temperature, and salinity.

Researchers use ARGO to track upper-ocean heat content, freshwater changes, stratification, and basin-scale variability.
For robust analysis, quality-control filters should be applied before building climatologies or anomaly products.
